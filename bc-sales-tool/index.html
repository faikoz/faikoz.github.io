Exploratory analytical views — each chart answers a different question about your sales than the operational KPIs on Overview. All views derive from the toolbar's loaded set.

Sales flow · salesperson → category

click a salesperson on the left to highlight only their flows · click a category on the right to drill into Sales by Item · click again to clear

Sales hierarchy · sunburst

inner ring = salesperson (click to highlight) · outer ring = item category (click to drill into Sales by Item) · hover for revenue

Salesperson performance radar

6 normalised axes · larger polygon = stronger overall · spikes show specialisation

Sales pipeline funnel

documents at each stage · conversion % shown between stages · click any bar to view the underlying customers / invoices on Overview

Monthly revenue ridges per category

each ridge = one top category · curve shape = month-by-month sales trajectory · stacked vertically · taller ridge = higher peak

Sales treemap · category → item

area = sales · colour = GP% (red < 0% · orange 0–10% · amber 10–20% · light green 20–30% · dark green 30%+) · click to drill into Sales by Item

Customer concentration

how lopsided your customer base is — a few big fish vs many small ones
Top 20% of customers drive…

Curve bowed below the dashed line = concentrated. Straight diagonal = perfectly even spread across all customers.

Item bubble matrix

X = qty · Y = avg unit price · size = revenue · colour = category

Period-over-period waterfall

left bar = prior period total · floating bars show what added (green) or subtracted (red) · right bar = current period

Top customers · slope chart

prior period (left) vs current (right) · slope = growth direction

Seasonality polar chart

each ring = one year · spikes show busy months · rings overlapping = consistent seasonal pattern · rings diverging = year-over-year drift

Invoice value distribution

how big are your typical invoices · X-axis = invoice $ (log scale) · bar height = number of invoices in that $ band · title shows median / mean / P90

RFM customer segmentation

X = days since last purchase (recency, log) · Y = # invoices (frequency) · bubble size = total spend · colour = segment

Profit density heatmap · customer × category

rows sorted by total revenue · cols sorted by total category revenue · cell colour = GP \$ (dark green high profit · red loss) · hover for exact \$

Customer lifecycle stacked area

customer count per month · New / Active (≤3 mo) / At-Risk (3–6 mo) / Churned (6+ mo) · click a month to list customers below

Geographic sales · Australia by state

state fill intensity = revenue · click a state to highlight · hover for details

Top customers · sparkline grid

Each tile = one customer · big number = their total revenue in the toolbar range · smoothed curve below = monthly trajectory (full loaded history) · arrow + Δ% = current month vs first month

Daily sales calendar heatmap

click a day to drill in · use the back button to restore the previous range · dark cells = high revenue · view scrolled to most-recent end

Customer cohort retention

how well each batch of new customers keeps buying · rows that stay dark moving right = sticky cohorts · rows that fade = customers churning

Every count is one unique Quote No. across the four Residential sources (active quotes + Quote Archive + Residential Sales Orders + Residential Posted Invoices). One quote that spawned 5 invoices = 1. Won = Quote No. has a Residential Order or Invoice, OR the active quote's Campaign No is QUOTE WON. Lost = active quote's Campaign No is LOST - AVAILABILITY / LOST - PRICE / LOST - PROJECT. Other = active quote with any other campaign (Follow Up / Open / Pending / Price Query / Live / WFB / Repricing / etc.) — sub-label kept in the by-campaign chart. Archived (historic) = Quote No. only in the archive (converted out of window, or deleted). Total = unique Quote Nos. in the archive — the denominator for Win rate.

Definitions for this view (limited to invoices in the loaded date range — for true "first-time-ever" data, load a wider range). New-in-month: a customer whose first appearance in the loaded data falls in that month. Returning-in-month: a customer active that month who already appeared in an earlier month within the loaded range. One-time customers: appeared in only one month across the loaded period. Repeat customers: appeared in two or more months. Lost customers: last purchase was more than 6 months before today. Click any month (in the chart or table) to see the customer list for that month.
